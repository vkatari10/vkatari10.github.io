Low-Earth Acessible Research Network Satellite (LEARNSat)

The SSRL has developed advanced satellite buses and kits, which are provided to high schools in the form of educational LEARNsat kits and the satellite bus itself. These kits offer students the unique opportunity to learn about satellite technology through hands-on experience. High school students, guided by detailed instructions and educational materials, are able to complete the assembly of the satellite bus on their own. This initiative aims to inspire and educate the next generation of engineers and scientists by offering practical exposure to space technology at an early stage in their academic careers.

- Lowers barriers to entering CubeSat technology

- Lowers costs for students

- Lowers technical requirements to manufacture buses

Ease of Use

With specialized satellite buses and educational kits, which are provided to high schools as part of an innovative outreach program. These satellite buses, along with the comprehensive LEARNsat educational kits, are designed to introduce students to the fundamentals of satellite technology and space engineering. By following the detailed instructions and utilizing the resources included in these kits, high school students are able to fully assemble the satellite bus themselves. This hands-on experience not only enhances their understanding of complex scientific concepts but also encourages them to explore potential careers in the fields of aerospace and engineering. Through this initiative, SSRL aims to empower young learners with the practical skills and knowledge needed to succeed in the rapidly evolving space industry.

Launch

Launched using weather balloons, this mission is designed to function as a suborbital mission, meaning it remains within Earth's atmosphere rather than traveling into space. By avoiding the complexities of space-bound launches, the entire process becomes significantly more manageable and straightforward to execute. This suborbital approach eliminates the need for advanced rocket systems and complex launch infrastructure, making the mission more cost-effective and less technically challenging. Additionally, the simplified process reduces the logistical and regulatory hurdles typically associated with space launches, allowing for a quicker and more accessible way to conduct scientific experiments, educational programs, or technology demonstrations at high altitudes. This approach opens the door for a broader range of participants, from schools to research institutions, to engage in near-space exploration without the high costs and risks involved in traditional space missions.
